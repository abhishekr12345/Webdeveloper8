Kishkindha Kanda

Book IV of the Ramayana

Kishkindha Kanda is the fourth book of the ancient Indian epic, the Ramayana, composed by Sage Valmiki. This section narrates the adventures of Lord Rama in the kingdom of the Vanaras (monkey-like beings) and his alliance with Hanuman and Sugriva.

Main Events

  • Rama and Lakshmana arrive in Kishkindha searching for Sita.
  • They meet Hanuman, who introduces them to Sugriva.
  • Rama helps Sugriva defeat his brother Vali and regain the throne.
  • Sugriva sends his army to search for Sita, who was abducted by Ravana.
  • Hanuman leaps across the ocean to reach Lanka and finds Sita in Ashoka Vatika.

Key Characters

  • Rama – The hero of the epic, searching for his wife Sita.
  • Hanuman – Devoted servant of Rama and son of the wind god.
  • Sugriva – Exiled king of Kishkindha, later restored by Rama.
  • Vali – Sugriva’s brother and rival.
  • Sita – Rama’s wife, held captive in Lanka.

Significance

This Kanda marks the beginning of Rama’s alliance with the Vanaras, especially Hanuman, whose devotion and bravery become central to the epic. The events set the stage for the eventual battle in Lanka.

“Where there is devotion, there is strength.” – Kishkindha Kanda
