Sundara Kanda

Book V of the Ramayana

Sundara Kanda is the fifth book of the epic Ramayana, composed by Sage Valmiki. It is considered the most beautiful and spiritually uplifting section, focusing on Hanuman’s journey to Lanka in search of Sita.

Main Events

  • Hanuman leaps across the ocean to reach Lanka.
  • He encounters and defeats various obstacles, including Surasa and Simhika.
  • Hanuman explores Lanka and finds Sita in Ashoka Vatika.
  • He delivers Rama’s message to Sita and reassures her.
  • Hanuman destroys part of Lanka and defeats many demons.
  • He is captured by Ravana but escapes after setting Lanka ablaze.
  • Hanuman returns to Rama with news of Sita’s location.

Key Characters

  • Hanuman – The central hero of Sundara Kanda, known for his strength and devotion.
  • Sita – Rama’s wife, held captive by Ravana in Lanka.
  • Ravana – Demon king of Lanka, antagonist of the epic.
  • Surasa – Sea goddess who tests Hanuman’s courage.
  • Vibhishana – Ravana’s righteous brother who later allies with Rama.

Significance

Sundara Kanda is revered for its spiritual depth and emotional power. Hanuman’s unwavering devotion and bravery inspire readers to overcome obstacles with faith and determination. Many devotees recite this Kanda for strength and blessings.
