Ayodhya Kanda

The Second Book of the Ramayana – In English

Overview

Ayodhya Kanda is the second chapter of the ancient Indian epic Ramayana, composed by Sage Valmiki. It tells the story of Lord Rama's exile and the emotional events in the kingdom of Ayodhya.

Key Events

  • King Dasaratha decides to crown Rama as the next king.
  • Kaikeyi demands Rama's exile and Bharata's coronation.
  • Rama accepts exile; Sita and Lakshmana go with him.
  • Dasaratha dies in grief after Rama leaves.
  • Bharata refuses the throne and places Rama’s sandals on it.

Philosophical Themes

  • Dharma and duty above personal desire
  • Loyalty and sacrifice in relationships
